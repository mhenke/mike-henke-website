# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech recruiters, hiring managers, and technical decision-makers evaluating Mike Henke for senior engineering and leadership roles. They land on this site after seeing his name in a job application, LinkedIn profile, or conference talk. They spend 60-90 seconds forming a first impression; if it passes, they read deeper into blog posts.

## Product Purpose

Establish Mike as a credible senior technologist with 20+ years of experience who has delivered significant enterprise value ($1.8M at Union Pacific). The site must communicate: I build things that work, I lead teams that ship, I'm not a commodity developer.

## Brand Personality

**Confident & Bold** — Direct, no-overselling, technical credibility over marketing speak. The design should feel like a senior engineer built it: clean, purposeful, not trying too hard. Not flashy for flashy's sake; bold when boldness serves the message.

## Anti-references

- AI slop: generic gradient hero backgrounds, stock photo beach/desk setups, "Hello, I'm a Full Stack Developer" prose
- Cookie-cutter portfolio templates with timeline cards, icon grids, and "passionate about code" statements
- Dark-theme-with-neon-accent developer portfolios that look like every other Vercel/Next.js template
- Unspecific testimonials ("Mike is amazing!") without concrete enterprise context

## Design Principles

1. **Technical credibility through restraint** — Every element earns its place. No decorative clutter. The design demonstrates the same engineering values the content claims: clean architecture, no unnecessary complexity.
2. **Enterprise weight without corporate stiffness** — This is a $1.8M enterprise deliverable, not a side project. The site should feel like it came from someone who builds production systems, not someone who builds portfolio sites.
3. **Show the work, not the process** — Timeline and skills sections exist to contextualize achievements, not to demonstrate methodology theater. Concrete outcomes over abstract competencies.
4. **Dark theme as professional default** — The dark theme signals "I work in terminals and IDEs." It's the natural habitat of a senior engineer. Not edgy or countercultural — just honest.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum, target AAA where practical
- Reduced motion respected (already implemented in CSS)
- Keyboard navigation fully functional (skip links, focus states)
- Screen reader landmarks in place (already implemented)
- No reliance on color alone for information (already has proper contrast ratios)
